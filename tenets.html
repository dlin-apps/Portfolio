UX & DESIGN TENETS

Over the years I have developed a set of guiding principles for my approach to product strategy, vision setting, people management, and execution.

01

IT'S A DESIGN DEMOCRACY. TRUST YOUR ELECTED OFFICIALS.

Good ideas can come from anywhere. UX teams should champion great ideas regardless of source.

02

TOP-DOWN MEETS BOTTOMS-UP

UX teams only succeed when creating compelling vision while breaking things into executable pieces.

03

BE THE PERSON WHO MAKES THINGS SIMPLER

Making things simple is the hardest and most valuable skill. Don't dazzle with complexity.

04

FOCUS ON USERS AND SCENARIOS, NOT FEATURES

What job did customers hire your product for? Start with what users really want.

05

IF USERS CAN'T FIND IT, IT DOESN'T EXIST

Discovery and first experience are key to successful products.

06

INTERNALIZE PROCESS TO KNOW WHAT TO SKIP

Great UX professionals know what they can skip and when.

07

IF NO ONE COMPLAINS, THE BAR ISN'T HIGH ENOUGH

Compromises are likely being made too quickly in favor of implementation over user experience.

08

DEEP DOMAIN KNOWLEDGE IS REQUIRED

Designers need technical training, sales exposure, and competitive landscape knowledge.

09

IT'S ALL ABOUT USER PERCEPTION

User perception is the only effective way to measure UX success.

10

CONVINCE A BUYER TO BUY, A USER TO RENEW

Target buyer personas to get customers on board. Convince users to renew.

11

ALL GOOD THINGS START WITH PSYCHOLOGICAL SAFETY

The ability for anyone to feel safe to speak their mind is crucial to any well-functioning team.

12

BE CLEAR, NOT CLEVER

Clarity delivers great products and teams that collaborate well over time.
