Philosophy

GUIDING
PRINCIPLES

Over the years I have developed a set of guiding principles for my approach to product strategy, vision setting, people management, and execution.

01

It's a Design Democracy

Trust your elected officials. Great design emerges from collaboration, not isolation. Everyone contributes, but decisions need clear ownership.

02

Challenge the Status Quo

I want designers who show a bit of ego and cause some trouble when appropriate. There should be healthy tension that pushes innovation.

03

Passion for the Craft

I look for passion for design of any type. People who care deeply about their work create experiences that users remember.

04

Tell Stories

You can't be a great UX Designer without being a great presenter and storyteller. Getting your idea across and inspiring others is crucial.

05

Embrace Complexity

Most UX Designers work on complex systems. I look for skills and aptitude to deal with complexity—filtering, workflows, and states.

06

Be Transparent

Transparency about contributions matters. Acknowledge the team, be clear about your role, and build trust through honesty.
